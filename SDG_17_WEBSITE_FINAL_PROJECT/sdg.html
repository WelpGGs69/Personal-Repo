What are the Sustainable Development Goals?


The Sustainable Development Goals are the blueprint to achieve a better and more sustainable future for all. They address the global challenges we face, including those related to poverty, inequality, climate change, environmental degradation, peace and justice. The 17 Goals are all interconnected, and in order to leave no one behind, it is important that we achieve them all by 2030.


People

End poverty and hunger in all forms and ensure dignity and equality for all people.

Planet

Protect our planet's natural resources and climate for future generations.

Prosperity

Ensure prosperous and fulfilling lives in harmony with nature for all.

History

Adopted by all UN members in 2015, the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development offers a common framework for future peace and prosperity for both people and the environment. The 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), which are an urgent call to action for all nations‚Äîdeveloped and developing‚Äîin a global partnership, are at its core. They understand that efforts to combat climate change and protect our oceans and forests must coexist with measures that enhance health and education, lessen inequality, and promote economic growth in order to eradicate poverty and other forms of deprivation.

The worldwide development initiatives that started with the Millennium Development Goals (MDGs) in 2000 were continued and improved with the creation of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). The MDGs were narrowly focused and did not address more general global issues like inequality or climate change. Instead, they concentrated primarily on poverty, health, and education in poor nations.

Acknowledging these disparities, world leaders convened at the Rio+20 Conference in 2012 and decided to create a new set of objectives including social, economic, and environmental issues that would be applicable to all countries. The 17 SDGs were formally accepted in 2015 by 193 UN member states as part of the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development following three years of international consultations including governments, scientists, organizations, and citizens.

Our Vision

A world empowered by collaborative action to accomplish all Sustainable Development Goals for a fair and sustainable future, where everyone lives in peace, equality, and environmental harmony.

Our Mission

To educate, motivate, and inspire communities by offering easily accessible information, interesting resources, and worthwhile experiences that encourage awareness, action, and teamwork in the pursuit of the Sustainable Development Goals.
